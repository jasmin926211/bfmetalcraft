FAQs

What are deep drawn components?

Deep drawn presswork is by no means a simple process. Typically, deep drawing is considered as when the depth of the drawn parts exceeds its diameter. This depth is attained by anneling and redrawing the component through a series of dies until the component attain the final shape.

Which material can be deep drawn?

Appropriate materials include aluminum copper, aluminium, brass, rolled low carbon sheet steel, and stainless steels.

What is deep drawn stainless steel (S.S)?

Deep drawing is the process of forming sheet metal by using a punch to radially draw the metal into a forming die. The mechanical action of the punch in combination with the hollow die applies both tensile and compressive forces that transform the shape under high pressure.
